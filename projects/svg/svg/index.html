Where the River Runs

by Morten Rand-Hendriksen, Published June 4, 2016, Tags: thoughts, rivers

From our house in Lillehammer, we could see Mj√∏sa, the largest lake in Norway. in the summer it would be a calm sheet of blue speckled with the white sails of boats. In the winter, the lake froze, as if someone had covered the water in a blanket. Flying over the ice, we'd see speed skaters and dare devils in their cars. During the Olympic Games, the lake was used as a parking lot, all traces of it lost as warmer weather melted the blanket and revealed, once again, the cool waters of the past, brought by the river from ancient glaciers.
